The problem

Manufacturers forecast by the rear-view mirror.

Demand in Latin American construction is real and valuable - but it's buried in customs records, public tenders, port manifests, and satellite imagery that no one has ever assembled into a single line. So planners fall back on what they can see: last quarter's sales.

  • ✕Lagging. Sales reports describe demand that already happened - the order is already placed, or already lost to a competitor.
  • ✕Incomplete. A buyer stitches a syndicated PDF, free national stats, and a price feed into a picture that's stale before it's finished.
  • ✕Coarse. The few forward forecasts that exist stop at the country level - useless for deciding which plant runs which SKU next month.

Why now

Three forces just converged.

The forecast was impossible to build a few years ago. It isn't anymore.

01

Data finally machine-readable

Customs portals, open-contracting tender feeds, and free Sentinel-2 imagery became queryable at the same time. The raw signal is now reachable - it just isn't assembled.

02

AI can fuse it

Modern models read satellite masks, parse messy tender text, and align mismatched sources into one coherent forecast - work that was manually impossible at this scale.

03

Volatility makes it urgent

Latin American construction demand swings hard on tenders, trade, and cycles. Planning against last quarter is more expensive than it has ever been.

Market & vision

A cheap wedge into a $500M+ data market.

No one sells a structured, forward, sub-country construction-demand signal for Latin America - the only product that does this anywhere is US-only. We start where the signal is richest and the incumbents are thinnest, then let the data asset compound.

The same fused signal ports to new geographies, new buyers, and new products - the path to becoming the Argus / Wood Mackenzie-style data layer for the building-materials economy across emerging markets.
